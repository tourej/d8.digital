The Real Risk

What Happens
When You Don't.

Most website emergencies are preventable. Hacked sites, corrupted databases, broken checkouts, and disappearing content, the vast majority trace back to one thing: a site that was built and then forgotten.

The businesses that pay for an emergency rebuild are almost always the ones who were saving money by not paying for maintenance. The math never works in their favor.

  • Outdated plugins are the #1 vector for WordPress site hacks
  • Average cost of a hacked website recovery: $2,000–$10,000
  • A site without backups that crashes loses everything, content, customers, history
  • Unmaintained CMS installs run slower month over month as technical debt compounds
  • Security gaps create liability, especially for sites that process payments or hold customer data
