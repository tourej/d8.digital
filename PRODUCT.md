# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Houston and Manila-based small and medium business owners (retail, health/wellness, professional services, nonprofits, authors, food/beverage) who need a digital agency partner. They arrive comparison-shopping or via referral. They are not technical. They need to trust the agency's expertise fast. Decision-makers scanning on mobile during the workday; they don't read long pages, they feel them.

## Product Purpose

D8.Digital is the marketing and portfolio site for a boutique digital agency (formerly Demonstr8d Solutions), headquartered in Houston TX with a second office in Manila, PH. The site must win trust at first impression, demonstrate expertise through the work, and convert visitors into booked discovery calls. Success is a completed booking or a direct email from a qualified lead.

## Brand Personality

Expert. Direct. Fearless.

Voice is confident without arrogance. Copy earns its place — no filler, no hedging. The brand says what it does, shows what it's done, and lets the results speak. Tone is peer-to-peer with business owners, not agency-speak to clients.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS**: pastel blues, hero metric blocks (big number + small label), identical card grids, default Webflow aesthetics. This site must feel human, not software.
- **Agency award-bait**: hyper-animated cursor-follower portfolios that impress other designers but confuse clients. Motion serves meaning, not spectacle.
- **Corporate bloat**: stock photography, committee copy, 14-item nav, Fortune 500 agency stiffness. D8 is a boutique; it should feel like one.
- **Template shops**: any off-the-shelf aesthetic (ThemeForest, WordPress starter kits). Every design decision should feel made, not selected.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what we preach.** The site IS the proof. If we sell web design, the site must be the best-designed thing a visitor has ever seen from an agency at this scale. No exceptions.
2. **Earn trust through specificity.** Vague claims ("we help you grow") lose to specific results ("89% revenue increase, first month"). Every section should prefer the concrete over the abstract.
3. **Dark is a statement, not a default.** The dark palette signals premium, after-hours hustle, screen-native confidence. It should feel chosen, not trendy.
4. **Green is earned.** The brand green (`oklch(72% 0.185 145)`) is the most expressive element on the page. It marks what matters: CTAs, active states, accents, results. Diluting it with overuse breaks the signal.
5. **No feature orphans.** Every page and section should be reachable from somewhere and earn its place. Dead-end experiences, unsurfaced pages, and stranded navigation items signal a business that doesn't sweat the details.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline. Custom cursor is decorative only (system cursor accessible via `cursor: none` on body is acceptable for desktop, ensure pointer cursor on mobile). Color contrast: green on dark bg passes AA. Reduced-motion: honor `prefers-reduced-motion` by disabling animation reveals and cursor ring.
